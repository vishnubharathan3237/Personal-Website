Science and Math UIL

Science UIL: Being in Science UIL challenged me to build knowledge in AP Biology, AP Chemistry, and AP Physics while learning how to apply concepts under pressure. I worked with a group to prepare for the competition, and we organized study sessions which helped strengthen everyone’s understanding. Because of the preparation, I competed at the district level and gained experience in both teamwork and academic problem-solving.

Math UIL: Out of the three options in Math UIL: Calculator, Mathematics, and Number Sense, I volunteered for the Calculator section because I had experience in it and no experience in Number Sense or Mathematics. This competition allowed me to grow, not only in Maths, but also as a group member. With my group, we all came up with strategies to get far in the competition.

ThinkNeuro Software Engineering Internship

Before ThinkNeuro, coding was always a solo hobby or just a class. But this internship showed me what it’s actually like to build something with other people. I stopped just writing code and started collaborating. Learning Git was another big help from this internship. It showed me how to cleanly organize code without it getting messy and distracting. Because of multiple workshops and lessons, it finally ended with a working iOS app.

Although this internship helped me make an app and work with people. It also pushed me to research and learn different things, such as how to use Python, Java, and C.

First Lego League | Engineering Lead

This competition required members to use strong knowledge of both coding and building with legos to create a fully functional robot. The robot had to do a certain amount of tasks in a given amount of time. The only way to do that was to design and code a robot that would work in the designated amount of time without breaking. As Engineering Lead in my group, I had to instruct and lead other people to design and build the robot while also fitting it to the coding team's requirements.
